Corruption

The NLD government has made some progress in tackling growing inequality and rising business costs in Myanmar, but corruption remains endemic in both the public and private sectors, and is sure to be a pressing issue for the next administration. The NLD has toughened the legal and institutional mechanisms to combat graft, and several government officials and company directors have been prosecuted for corruption and bribery.

The biggest catch was Lei Lei Maw, chief minister of Tanintharyi region and an NLD member, sentenced to 30 years on bribery charges. Myanmar advanced two positions this year on the Transparency International Corruption Perception Index – but it’s still ranked 130th out of 180 countries, alongside Mexico and communist-governed Laos.
