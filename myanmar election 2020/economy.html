Economy and poverty

Myanmar’s economy has grown at an annual rate of around 6 percent during the NLD’s time in office, but the COVID-19 pandemic and resultant government-ordered lockdown are expected to all but wipe out GDP growth for the current fiscal year. This threatens recent progress in poverty reduction while shrinking incomes of households.

The crisis has especially affected wholesale and retail trade, tourism-related services, manufacturing, and construction. It also has disrupted agricultural production and food supplies. In addition, factory closures due to the pandemic in neighboring China and Thailand have forced tens of thousands of Myanmar migrant workers to return home and try to make ends meet in Myanmar’s agricultural sector.

The NLD government’s COVID-19 Economic Relief Plan has helped mitigate the impact on businesses and individuals. Donor nations have deferred some debt payments and provided aid. Sustaining recovery will be a key challenge for the next government although analysts anticipate the economy will bounce back after the pandemic ends.
