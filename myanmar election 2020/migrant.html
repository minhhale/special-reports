Migrant laborers and refugees

The numbers of ethnic minority people displaced by conflict has only climbed under the NLD government. In addition to the hundreds of thousands of Rohingya displaced internally and in Bangladesh, some 200,000 mostly ethnic Rakhines have been uprooted by the current conflict in Rakhine State. They now shelter in temporary camps and Buddhist monasteries and face restrictions on access to humanitarian assistance. To the north and east, there are nearly 100,000 displaced civilians in Kachin state and roughly 10,000 others housed in camps in northern Shan. About 100,000 more remain in long-term camps along the Thai border that were established more than three decades ago.

A new challenge for Myanmar is the increasingly complicated plight of Myanmar migrant workers who have long faced hardships in low-paying jobs in Thailand and China. More than 110,000 migrant workers have returned since March, mostly from Thailand, because of the COVID-19 pandemic, according to the International Organization for Migration. Impoverished workers were holed up for weeks under a government-ordered quarantine upon their arrival in Myanmar and have had to scrape by on farming since returning to their home villages and can no longer send home the remittances from their earnings overseas. Many will now be looking to go back to work in the neighboring countries.
